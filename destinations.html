Mount Rainier

Mount Rainier is a towering stratovolcano in Washington State, standing at 14,411 feet, making it the highest peak in the Cascade Range. Known for its massive glaciers, wildflower-filled meadows, and diverse ecosystems, it’s a popular destination for climbers, hikers, and nature enthusiasts. As an active volcano within Mount Rainier National Park, it offers a unique blend of beauty and geological significance.

Mount Saint Helens

Mount Saint Helens is an active stratovolcano located in Washington State, best known for its catastrophic eruption in 1980, which reshaped the landscape and created a massive crater. The eruption, one of the most significant volcanic events in U.S. history, caused widespread destruction and left lasting environmental impacts. Today, it remains a popular destination for hiking, research, and observing volcanic recovery and resilience in nature.

Hurricane Ridge

Hurricane Ridge is a stunning mountainous area located in Olympic National Park, Washington State, known for its breathtaking panoramic views of the Olympic Mountains. At 5,242 feet, it offers a range of outdoor activities, from hiking and wildlife spotting in the summer to snowshoeing and skiing in the winter. The area’s dramatic scenery and diverse ecosystems make it a favorite destination for nature lovers and adventurers alike.
